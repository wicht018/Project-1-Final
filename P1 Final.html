GIRL POWER

EXAMINING THE GENDER GAP IN ELITE GYMNASTICS

Every four years, the Olympics brings the otherwise obscure sport of gymnastics into America’s national spotlight. At this summer’s games in Rio, five young women – Simone Biles, Aly Raisman, Gabby Douglas, Laurie Hernandez, and Madison Kocian – completed the most dominant team performance in US history by winning 9 of 11 possible medals. In less than three months, they went from virtual unknowns to the beloved “Final Five.” Yet the US men - Sam Mikulak, Chris Brooks, Jake Dalton, Alex Naddour, and Danell Leyva - were decidedly less popular, despite improving upon their London struggles with three medals overall. These disparities are illustrated in the adjacent area graphs using Google Trends data. Click the timeline below to change the date displayed. So why, in a country where male athletes almost always overshadow the accomplishments of female athletes, does gymnastics see the opposite occur? Well, it appears there’s only one thing that appeals to an American fanbase which only watches gymnasts three weeks in the summer – winning.
